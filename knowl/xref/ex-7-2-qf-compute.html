Example 7.24.

Compute \(Q_A(\vec x) = \vec x^{T}A\vec x\) and \(Q_B(\vec x) = \vec x^{T}B\vec x\) for the following matrices.
🔗
(a)
\(A=\begin{bmatrix}4 \amp 0\\ 0 \amp 3\end{bmatrix}\)
🔗
Solution.
Note that
\begin{align*} Q_A(\vec x) \amp = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \amp x_2 \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} 4 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp 3 \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix}\\ \amp = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \amp x_2 \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} 4x_1 \\ 3x_2 \end{bmatrix}\\ \amp = x_1(4x_1) + x_2(3x_2)\\ \amp = 4x_1^2 + 3x_2^2 \end{align*}
🔗
🔗
🔗
(b)
\(B=\begin{bmatrix}4 \amp 1\\ 1 \amp -3\end{bmatrix}\)
🔗
Solution.
Note that
\begin{align*} Q_B(\vec x) \amp = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \amp x_2 \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} 4 \amp 1 \\ 1 \amp -3 \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix}\\ \amp = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \amp x_2 \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} 4x_1 + x_2 \\ x_1 - 3x_2 \end{bmatrix}\\ \amp = x_1(4x_1 + x_2) + x_2(x_1 - 3x_2)\\ \amp = 4x_1^2 + x_1x_2 + x_1x_2 - 3x_2^2\\ \amp = 4x_1^2 + 2x_1x_2 - 3x_2^2 \end{align*}
🔗
