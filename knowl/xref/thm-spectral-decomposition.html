Theorem 7.19. Spectral Decomposition.

Suppose \(A \) can be orthogonally diagonalized as
\begin{equation*} A=PDP^T = \begin{bmatrix} \vec u_1 \amp \cdots \amp \vec u_n\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \lambda_1 \amp \cdots \amp 0 \\ \vdots \amp \ddots \amp \vdots \\ 0 \amp \cdots \amp \lambda_n \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}\vec u_1^T \\ \vdots \\ \vec u_n^T \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Then \(A\) has the decomposition
\begin{equation*} A=\sum_{i=1}^n \lambda_i \vec u_i \vec u_i^T = \lambda_1 \vec u_1\vec u_1^T + \cdots + \lambda_n \vec u_n\vec u_n^T\text{.} \end{equation*}
Note that each term \(\lambda_i\vec u_i\vec u_i^T\) is a rank 1 matrix.
