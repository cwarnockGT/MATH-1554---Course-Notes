Theorem 6.30. Expansion in an Orthonormal Basis.

If \(\{\vec u_1,\dots,\vec u_p\}\) is orthonormal basis for \(W\) and \(\vec w\in W\text{,}\) then
🔗
\begin{equation*} \vec w = (\vec w\cdot\vec u_1)\vec u_1 + \cdots + (\vec w\cdot\vec u_p)\vec u_p, \end{equation*}
and
🔗
\begin{equation*} \|\vec w\| = \sqrt{ (\vec w\cdot\vec u_1)^2 + \cdots + (\vec w\cdot\vec u_p)^2 }. \end{equation*}
Solution.
Proof: The first result is a direct corollary of Theorem 6.23 since for each \(k \in \{1,2, \dots, p\}\text{,}\) we have that \(\vec u_k \cdot \vec u_k = 1\) because \(\vec u_k\) is a unit vector.
