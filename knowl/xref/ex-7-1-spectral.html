Example 7.20.

Construct a spectral decomposition of \(A\text{,}\) given its orthogonal diagonalization:
🔗
\begin{equation*} A=\begin{bmatrix}3 \amp 1\\ 1 \amp 3\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \tfrac1{\sqrt2} \amp -\tfrac1{\sqrt2}\\ \tfrac1{\sqrt2} \amp \tfrac1{\sqrt2} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}4 \amp 0\\ 0 \amp 2\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \tfrac1{\sqrt2} \amp \tfrac1{\sqrt2}\\ -\tfrac1{\sqrt2} \amp \tfrac1{\sqrt2} \end{bmatrix}. \end{equation*}
Solution.
Using the formula inTheorem 7.19, we have that
\begin{align*} A \amp = \lambda_1\vec u_1\vec u_1^T + \lambda_2\vec u_2\vec u_2^T\\ \amp = 4\begin{bmatrix}\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \\ \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \amp \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\end{bmatrix} + 2 \begin{bmatrix}\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \\ -\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \amp -\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\end{bmatrix}\\ \amp = 4 \begin{bmatrix} \frac{1}{2} \amp \frac{1}{2} \\ \frac{1}{2} \amp \frac{1}{2} \end{bmatrix} + 2\begin{bmatrix} \frac{1}{2} \amp -\frac{1}{2} \\ -\frac{1}{2} \amp \frac{1}{2} \end{bmatrix}\\ \amp = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \amp 2 \\ 2 \amp 2 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp -1 \\ -1 \amp 1 \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{align*}
Note that the first term in the sum is a fairly close approximation of \(A\text{.}\) In fact, it is the best rank 1 approximation of \(A\text{.}\)
