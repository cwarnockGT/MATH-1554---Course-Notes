Theorem 6.56. Unique Least Squares Solutions.

For an \(m\times n\) matrix \(A\text{,}\) the following are equivalent:
🔗
  1. For every \(\vec b\text{,}\) the equation \(A\vec x=\vec b\) has a unique least squares solution.
    🔗
  2. The columns of \(A\) are linearly independent.
    🔗
  3. \(A^TA\) is invertible.
    🔗
When these hold,
🔗
\begin{equation*} \widehat x = (A^TA)^{-1}A^T\vec b. \end{equation*}
