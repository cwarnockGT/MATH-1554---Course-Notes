Theorem 7.3. Convergence of Regular Stochastic Matrices.

If \(P\) is a regular \(m\times m\) stochastic matrix with \(m\ge2\) and with unique steady-state vector \(\vec q\text{,}\) then:
🔗
  1. There is a stochastic matrix \(\Pi\) such that
    \begin{equation*} \lim_{n\to\infty}P^n=\Pi\text{.} \end{equation*}
    🔗
    🔗
  2. For any starting probability vector \(\vec x_0\text{,}\) we have that
    \begin{equation*} \lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}P^n\vec x_0 = \vec q\text{.} \end{equation*}
    🔗
  3. Each column of \(\Pi\) equals \(\vec q\text{.}\)
    🔗
  4. \(\vec q\) is the unique eigenvector with eigenvalue \(1\text{.}\)
    🔗
  5. All eigenvalues of \(P\) satisfy \(|\lambda| \leq 1\text{.}\)
    🔗
Solution.
First, note that (2) is guaranteed since \(P\) is regular and so has a unique steady state vector \(\vec q\) regardless of the starting probability vector \(\vec x_0\text{.}\) We can prove (3) by observing that for any \(i = 1, \dots, n\text{,}\) we have that the \(i^\text{th}\) column of \(\Pi\) is given by
\begin{equation*} \Pi\vec e_i = \lim_{n\rightarrow \infty} P^n e_i = \vec q \end{equation*}
by (2). Hence, every column of \(\Pi\) is equal to \(\vec q\text{.}\)
