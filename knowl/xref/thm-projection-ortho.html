Theorem 6.26.

Let \(\vec u\) be a nonzero vector. Then, the vector \(\proj_{\vec u}\vec v\) is the vector in \(\Span\{\vec u\}\) which is closest to \(\vec v\text{.}\) Also, the vector \(\vec w=\vec v - \operatorname{proj}_{\vec u}(\vec v)\) is orthogonal to \(\vec u\text{.}\) Furthermore,
\begin{equation*} \|\vec v\|^2 = \|\proj_{\vec u}{\vec v}\|^2 + \|\vec w\|^2\text{.} \end{equation*}
