Example 6.45.

Let \(W = \Span\left\{\begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 0 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 8 \\ 5 \\ -6 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\text{.}\) Find an orthogonal basis for \(W\text{.}\)
🔗
Solution.
Let \(\vec x_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 0 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}\) and \(\vec x_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 8 \\ 5 \\ -6 \end{bmatrix}\text{.}\) Using Theorem 6.44, we have that
\begin{align*} \vec v_1 \amp = \vec x_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 0 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} \end{align*}
and
\begin{align*} \vec v_2 \amp = \vec x_2 - \proj_{\vec v_1}\vec x_2 \\ \amp = \begin{bmatrix} 8 \\ 5 \\ -6 \end{bmatrix} - \dfrac{8(3) + 5(0) -6(-1)}{3^2 + 0^2 + (-1)^2}\begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 0 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}\\ \amp = \begin{bmatrix}8 \\ 5 \\ -6 \end{bmatrix} - 3\begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 0 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}\\ \amp = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} \end{align*}
Note that \(\vec v_1, \vec v_2 \in W\) and \(\vec v_1 \cdot \vec v_2 = 0\text{,}\) so \(\left\{\vec v_1, \vec v_2\right\}\) is an orthogonal basis for \(W\text{.}\)
