    ðŸ”—
    \(\lambda\) is an eigenvalue of \(A\) if and only if \((A-\lambda I)\) is not invertible.
    ðŸ”—
    Therefore, to calculate the eigenvalues of \(A\text{,}\) we can need to find all \(\lambda\) so that
    ðŸ”—
    \begin{equation*} \det(A-\lambda I)=0. \end{equation*}

    Definition 5.13.

    The quantity \(\det(A-\lambda I)\) is the characteristic polynomial of \(A\text{.}\)
    ðŸ”—
    The equation \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\) is the characterisitc equation of \(A\text{.}\)
    ðŸ”—
    The roots of the characteristic polynomial are the eigenvalues of \(A\text{.}\)

    Definition 5.18. Geometric Multiplicity.

    The geometric multiplicity of an eigenvalue \(\lambda\) is the dimension of \(\operatorname{Null}(A-\lambda I)\text{.}\)
    ðŸ”—
    ðŸ”—
    1. Geometric multiplicity is always at least 1. It can be smaller than algebraic multiplicity.
      ðŸ”—
    2. Basic example:
      \begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix} 0 \amp 1\\ 0 \amp 0 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
      has only eigenvalue \(\lambda=0\text{.}\) Its algebraic multiplicity is 2, but its geometric multiplicity is 1.
